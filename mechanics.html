GameMechancis

Status: In-progress

This project tackles the ambiguity surrounding the term "game mechanics" within the academic and industry. Despite numerous definitions across various studies, no consensus exists on a single interpretation. By employing a mixed-method research approach researcher highlights the diversity and the need for clarity when using the term. Additionally, the project delves into the challenges of analyzing qualitative data—rich in detail but complex to sift through—for patterns and insights. Utilizing clustering techniques are applied to manage and interpret the qualitative data efficiently. This dual approach emphasizes the project's goal to bridge understanding between academic and industrial perspectives on the term game mechanics.

Role and Contributions

In this project, I took on the role of a UX researcher, employing mixed-method approaches to discern patterns within qualitative data. Alongside another researcher, I engaged in thematic coding to delineate various themes and sub-themes present in the data. To delve deeper into these patterns, we applied clustering techniques specifically tailored for qualitative data analysis. As the lead on this component, I developed Python scripts to process and analyze the data, and I was responsible for visualizing the results, thus enhancing our understanding of the underlying patterns within the data.

ResearchGoal

Our goal with study was uncover the conceptual overalps and divides exists for the nuanced interpretations of term “Game Mechanics” .
The goals of the research project were as follows:

  • Investigate how we, as a community understand and talk about ambiguous concepts like game mechanic
  • Investigate how the term ("Game Mechanics") is defined within the broader academic community or within the games industry
  • Identify the conceptual similarities and distinction in the various definitions

ResearchMethodology

For this study, I used a two research methods:

  • Phase 1 : Thematic Coding


    • The final dataset has 71 definitions that represent academic and industrial spheres. To analyze this dataset, we utilized thematic coding to categorize the definitions into 48 codes that were grouped into eight themes.

  • Phase 2: Clustering (K-means) for Qualitative Data

    • To employ a clustering algorithm, I repurposed the binary sequence as a 48-dimensional binary vector, each vector representing one definition. Utilizing the Kmeans algorithm, I partitioned the dataset into 'k' clusters, with each cluster composed of vectors (representing definitions). Clusters were generated for K values spanning from 3 to 11.

Analysis and Synthesis

  • Phase 1: Analysis of Thematic Coding

    • To analyze this dataset, we first used an iterative open coding approach. One researcher iterated over the definitions and found relevant or significant phrases or terms, such as “rules” or “player actions”, as used in the definitions, and each new term created a new code. This was at first a very open coding without predetermined categories or dimensions in mind. This generated around 45 individual codes, of several different types and dimensions,and of varying degrees of detail. A second coder then used these codes and re-coded all the definitions with this fixed codebook, tagging where appropriate, but not creating new codes. The two coders then used the disagreements found in the codes to discuss the sensibility and usefulness of each type of code, and they were thus iterated upon
